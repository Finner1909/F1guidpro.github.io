The Foundations of Braking

Grip & Weight Transfer

Braking shifts weight to the front tyres, temporarily increasing their grip. Mastering this transfer is key to finding the limit of adhesion.

Trail Braking

Gradually release the brake as you turn into a corner. This keeps the front tyres loaded, helping the car rotate and preventing understeer.

Brake Modulation (No ABS)

Apply 100% brake pressure at high speed, then smoothly decrease pressure as the car slows and downforce reduces to avoid locking up.

Dynamic Braking Window

Braking points are not static. They change with:

  • Fuel Load: Heavier car = earlier braking.
  • Tyre Wear: Worn tyres = less grip = earlier braking.
  • Weather: Wet conditions = much earlier braking.

Practice Plan

1. Isolate & Learn (5 Laps)

Brake 15-20m early. Focus only on perfecting the racing line without pressure.

2. Find the Limit (5 Laps)

Brake later in 5m increments until you miss the apex. This is your absolute limit.

3. Establish Consistency (10 Laps)

Dial back 5m from your limit. This is your repeatable race marker. Hit it consistently.

In-Lap Tuning

Forward Brake Bias (e.g., 57%)

More force to the front. Increases stability in heavy, straight-line braking but can cause understeer on turn-in.

Rearward Brake Bias (e.g., 54%)

More force to the rear. Helps the car rotate on corner entry but increases the risk of instability and rear locking.

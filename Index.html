            <h2>The Foundations of Braking</h2>
            <div class="info-grid">
                <div class="info-card"><h4>Grip & Weight Transfer</h4><p>Braking shifts weight to the front tyres, temporarily increasing their grip. Mastering this transfer is key to finding the limit of adhesion.</p></div>
                <div class="info-card"><h4>Trail Braking</h4><p>Gradually release the brake as you turn into a corner. This keeps the front tyres loaded, helping the car rotate and preventing understeer.</p></div>
                <div class="info-card"><h4>Brake Modulation (No ABS)</h4><p>Apply 100% brake pressure at high speed, then smoothly decrease pressure as the car slows and downforce reduces to avoid locking up.</p></div>
                <div class="info-card"><h4>Dynamic Braking Window</h4><p>Braking points are not static. They change with:</p><ul><li><strong>Fuel Load:</strong> Heavier car = earlier braking.</li><li><strong>Tyre Wear:</strong> Worn tyres = less grip = earlier braking.</li><li><strong>Weather:</strong> Wet conditions = much earlier braking.</li></ul></div>

            <h2>PS5 Controller Calibration</h2>
            <div class="info-grid">
                <div class="info-card"><h4>Steering Linearity: <span>40</span></h4><p>Softens initial steering to prevent twitchiness and promote smooth turn-in.</p></div>
                <div class="info-card"><h4>Throttle Linearity: <span>50</span></h4><p>Crucial for avoiding wheelspin. Makes the first half of the trigger pull less aggressive.</p></div>
                <div class="info-card"><h4>Brake Linearity: <span>35</span></h4><p>Helps prevent lock-ups by making the initial brake application more progressive.</p></div>
                <div class="info-card"><h4>Steering Rate: <span>130%</span></h4><p>Increases responsiveness for the short travel of an analog stick.</p></div>
                <div class="info-card"><h4>Max Wheel Rotation: <span>345°</span></h4><p>Reduces stick movement needed for full lock, making hairpins easier.</p></div>
                <div class="info-card"><h4>Vibration Strength: <span>120</span></h4><p>Enhances tactile feedback through triggers, crucial for sensing tyre slip and lock-ups.</p></div>

            <h2>Advanced Techniques</h2>
            <h3>Practice Plan</h3>
            <div class="info-grid">
                <div class="info-card"><h4>1. Isolate & Learn (5 Laps)</h4><p>Brake 15-20m early. Focus only on perfecting the racing line without pressure.</p></div>
                <div class="info-card"><h4>2. Find the Limit (5 Laps)</h4><p>Brake later in 5m increments until you miss the apex. This is your absolute limit.</p></div>
                <div class="info-card"><h4>3. Establish Consistency (10 Laps)</h4><p>Dial back 5m from your limit. This is your repeatable race marker. Hit it consistently.</p></div>
            </div>
            <h3>In-Lap Tuning</h3>
            <div class="info-grid">
                <div class="info-card"><h4>Forward Brake Bias (e.g., 57%)</h4><p>More force to the front. Increases stability in heavy, straight-line braking but can cause understeer on turn-in.</p></div>
                <div class="info-card"><h4>Rearward Brake Bias (e.g., 54%)</h4><p>More force to the rear. Helps the car rotate on corner entry but increases the risk of instability and rear locking.</p></div>
